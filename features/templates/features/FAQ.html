Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can we keep money in a conventional bank just for safety without using the interest?

Yes, keeping money in a conventional bank purely for safekeeping is allowed in necessity, provided the interest earned is neither used nor kept, but given away to charity without the intention of reward.

Reference: Mufti Taqi Usmani, Contemporary Fatawa
“If one is compelled to deposit money in an interest-based bank for security, the interest must be given away to the poor without seeking reward.”

Q: What should a Muslim do with interest money accidentally received?

Interest money should be disposed of by giving it to the poor or for public welfare, without the intention of earning reward, as it's not pure income.

Reference: Fatawa-e-Rahimiyyah, Vol. 3
"Such wealth should be discarded by giving it to the needy or in public welfare, without intention of sadaqah."

Q: If a Muslim’s salary is deposited in an interest-bearing account by default, what should they do?

The individual should transfer the salary to a Shariah-compliant account if available, and ensure any interest accumulated is removed and donated without expecting reward.

Reference: Majma’ al-Fiqh al-Islami (OIC), Resolution 1999
"One must separate clean earnings from unlawful additions and remove the latter."

Q: Is using interest money to pay government taxes or utility bills allowed?

No, using interest money to pay personal or obligatory dues like taxes or bills is not permissible. It should be given to the poor or for non-personal public use.

Reference: Fiqh al-Mu'amalat by Mufti Muhammad Shafi
"It is not permissible to use Riba-based income for personal obligations."

Q: Can a Shariah-compliant bank partner with a conventional bank for infrastructure (e.g., ATMs)?

Yes, as long as the partnership is limited to permissible services (e.g., cash withdrawal) and does not involve sharing in interest-based transactions.

Reference: AAOIFI Standard No. 6 (Shirkah)
“Permissible cooperation is allowed in areas where the core activity remains halal.”
